01

Project Summary

The proposal for Attic Pilates consists of creating an owned digital presence from scratch. The project aims to resolve the exclusive dependency on Instagram and the operational bottleneck of 100% manual WhatsApp bookings.

The project is structured in two sequential phases to give the studio a fast, well-designed digital space that functions as its central hub — from the visitor's first impression through to class booking.

02

Strategy & Objectives

Our solution directly addresses the three strategic objectives identified in Discovery.

Conversion

The current booking flow — WhatsApp message, manual confirmation, manual entry — does not scale. Every reservation requires direct team intervention, creating a bottleneck that limits growth and adds operational load. The primary objective of the site is to eliminate that friction entirely: clients should be able to discover a class, check availability, and confirm their spot without any back-and-forth. The site is not a brochure. It is a sales channel, and it needs to be designed and built as one — with booking as the primary CTA at every decision point.

Positioning

Attic has no owned digital presence beyond Instagram. For anyone who doesn't arrive through a direct referral — a Google search, a collaboration, a press mention — there is nothing to land on. No context, no credibility, no conversion. The website needs to close that gap from the first scroll. Every design decision, every line of copy, and every section of the site must communicate quality, intention, and technical expertise. The instructor team and the variety of disciplines are the core proof points — they need to be front and center, not buried.

Audience Expansion

The current client base is predominantly women aged 20–30 who arrive through Instagram. The studio wants to actively grow into a male and high-performance athletic segment — a demographic that evaluates differently. They respond to technical credibility, concrete outcomes, and a tone that is athletic rather than lifestyle-oriented. The site needs to speak to both audiences without diluting either. Visual language, copy tone, and class framing must be calibrated to bridge that gap — sporty enough to attract the new segment, refined enough to keep the existing one.

03

Project Scope

Phase A — Foundation
Full Website

The informational, brand, and acquisition layer. A responsive, mobile-first site that establishes Attic Pilates' owned digital presence.

  • Responsive web design and development (mobile-first priority)
  • Sections: Value proposition, Classes & Schedule (clearly differentiating Reformer, Barre, Cadillac and Yoga), About the Studio (Staff), Contact
  • On-page SEO — structure and metadata for Google visibility ("Pilates Palermo Soho")
  • Visual alignment: "Off-B&W" palette, Garet typography, and professional photography
Phase B — Owned Infrastructure
Proprietary Booking System

Replacement of the current manual flow with a native booking engine built directly into the studio's site — eliminating third-party dependency (SigueFit).

  • Client panel: view, book, and manage classes
  • Admin panel: schedules, classes, availability
  • Package and class credit management
  • Definitive elimination of the WhatsApp → manual entry flow
Exclusions — Out of Scope
Editorial Blog E-Commerce / Online Store Social Media Management Domain Registration Hosting

Information Architecture — Site Map

Proposed structure for a clean, conversion-focused, mobile-first website.

/   Home
/classes   Classes
/team   Team
/pricing   Pricing & Booking
/contact   Contact
  • 01
    Home

    Objective

    First impression and primary conversion point. Professional studio photography leads — the space does the selling. Value proposition in three sentences max, with a persistent booking CTA above the fold on mobile.

  • 02
    Classes

    Objective

    Inform and remove doubt. Clearly differentiate Reformer, Barre, Cadillac, and Yoga — and explain group vs. private. This page resolves the most common pre-booking hesitation: "Which class is right for me?"

  • 03
    Team

    Objective

    Build authority and trust. Instructor profiles are a declared differentiator — quality of the teaching staff is why clients choose Attic over a generic studio. This page makes that concrete.

  • 04
    Pricing & Booking

    Objective

    Convert intent into a confirmed reservation. Pack options are presented clearly, and the booking flow is embedded directly — no redirects, no friction. This is where the WhatsApp bottleneck gets eliminated.

  • 05
    Contact

    Objective

    Capture the undecided. Interactive map of Palermo Soho for local SEO and in-person discovery. Direct WhatsApp link for specific questions — kept as a secondary channel, not the default.
